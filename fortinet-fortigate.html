Living Off The Security Tools (LOTST)

Awareness for Fortinet FortiGate and similar platforms

⚠️ This page is for awareness and education only. It does not provide attack instructions.

Concept

Fortinet FortiGate is a legitimate enterprise firewall and security appliance, widely trusted for protecting networks against threats. Its deployments are common in enterprises and service providers. FortiGate is not malware. However, attackers may exploit the trust model by deploying their own FortiGate infrastructure and convincing organizations to route traffic through it.

How Abuse Could Look

  • The firewall is legitimate and configured correctly.
  • The attacker controls the FortiGate dashboard where traffic rules are managed.
  • Victims believe they are protected by a trusted network administrator.
  • The attacker can selectively allow or block traffic to hide their own activity.
  • This creates a false sense of security and long-term stealth.

Defensive Takeaways

  • Verify who controls the FortiGate dashboard your network connects to.
  • Ensure firewall instances are registered and managed under your organization’s infrastructure.
  • Audit firewall rules regularly for anomalies and hidden bypasses.
  • Treat unsolicited offers of “free firewall hosting” with caution.
  • Cross-check traffic logs with independent monitoring tools.
