Living Off The Security Tools (LOTST)

Awareness for CrowdStrike Falcon and similar platforms

⚠️ This page is for awareness and education only. It does not provide attack instructions.

Concept

CrowdStrike Falcon is a legitimate endpoint detection and response (EDR) platform, trusted by enterprises worldwide. Its agents are signed, widely deployed, and deeply integrated into operating systems. They are not malware. However, attackers may exploit the trust model by registering their own Falcon infrastructure and convincing organizations to connect agents to it.

How Abuse Could Look

  • The agent is legitimate and installed correctly.
  • The attacker controls the Falcon dashboard where the agent reports.
  • Victims believe they are monitored by a trusted security partner.
  • The attacker can selectively hide their own activity while reporting other attackers to appear legitimate.
  • This creates a false sense of security and long-term stealth.

Defensive Takeaways

  • Verify who controls the Falcon dashboard your agents connect to.
  • Ensure agents are registered under your organization’s account, not a third party.
  • Audit dashboards regularly for anomalies and hidden activity.
  • Treat unsolicited offers of “free monitoring” with caution.
  • Cross-check reported incidents with independent monitoring sources.
